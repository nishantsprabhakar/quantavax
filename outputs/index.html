The narrative

What if Bharat Biotech’s vaccine biology and validation infrastructure were paired with IBM’s quantum computing stack?

QuantaVax simulates that potential partnership as a discovery workflow. Bharat Biotech contributes pathogen knowledge, vaccine development experience, wet-lab validation, manufacturing context, and global health credibility. IBM contributes quantum hardware access, hybrid quantum-classical tooling, and research experience in mRNA secondary-structure optimization.

The app does not claim that such a partnership has been announced. It is a strategic simulator based on the concept note and current public research showing that quantum-assisted mRNA folding is moving from theory toward practical experiments.

Algorithm 01

QUBO-CVaR mRNA fold search

Base-pair constraints become a QUBO objective. A variational sampler searches low-energy folds, then a CVaR filter keeps the most stable tail of the distribution.

  1. Input: possible RNA base-pair decisions.
  2. QUBO: reward stable pairs and penalize impossible overlaps.
  3. CVaR: keep the best low-energy samples, not the noisy average.
  4. Output: fold candidates ranked by energy and feasibility.
Algorithm 02

VQE conformer confirmation

Antigen conformations are scored as energy states. Classical docking priors narrow the state space while quantum sampling explores hard-to-reach minima.

  1. Input: candidate antigen shapes and docking priors.
  2. Ansatz: prepare a parameterized quantum circuit.
  3. Loop: measure energy, update parameters, sample again.
  4. Output: low-energy conformers for antigen review.
Algorithm 03

Bayesian wet-lab triage

Structure stability, epitope exposure, manufacturability, and immune-receptor binding update a candidate posterior before validation slots are spent.

  1. Input: folding, epitope, binding, and lab-capacity signals.
  2. Prior: start with baseline candidate confidence.
  3. Update: raise or lower confidence as evidence arrives.
  4. Output: a shortlist for Bharat Biotech validation.

Model basis

The savings engine is a calibrated decision-support simulator. It estimates savings from fewer low-quality candidates entering wet-lab work, faster fold/conformer search, and higher validation throughput. It does not claim clinical efficacy or regulatory approval.

Reference anchors

IBM and Moderna reported quantum-assisted mRNA secondary-structure work at up to 60 nucleotides and 80 qubits. Bharat Biotech describes world-class R&D and manufacturing capabilities. The partnership in this app is hypothetical and intended to illustrate the concept note’s platform logic.
