Algorithm 01

QUBO-CVaR mRNA fold search

Base-pair constraints become a QUBO objective. A variational sampler searches low-energy folds, then a CVaR filter keeps the most stable tail of the distribution.

  1. Input: possible RNA base-pair decisions.
  2. QUBO: reward stable pairs and penalize impossible overlaps.
  3. CVaR: keep the best low-energy samples, not the noisy average.
  4. Output: fold candidates ranked by energy and feasibility.
Algorithm 02

VQE conformer confirmation

Antigen conformations are scored as energy states. Classical docking priors narrow the state space while quantum sampling explores hard-to-reach minima.

  1. Input: candidate antigen shapes and docking priors.
  2. Ansatz: prepare a parameterized quantum circuit.
  3. Loop: measure energy, update parameters, sample again.
  4. Output: low-energy conformers for antigen review.
Algorithm 03

Bayesian wet-lab triage

Structure stability, epitope exposure, manufacturability, and immune-receptor binding update a candidate posterior before validation slots are spent.

  1. Input: folding, epitope, binding, and lab-capacity signals.
  2. Prior: start with baseline candidate confidence.
  3. Update: raise or lower confidence as evidence arrives.
  4. Output: a shortlist for Bharat Biotech validation.

Model basis

The savings engine estimates the effect of better early triage: fewer weak candidates entering wet-lab work, faster fold and conformer search, and higher validation throughput. Boundary: this is not a clinical, regulatory, or manufacturing claim.
